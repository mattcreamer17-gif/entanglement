I. Context

Entanglement runs a deep-tech holding company that acts on two clocks at once. The parent closes partnerships on a commercial calendar: Oracle Cloud Infrastructure in May 2025, Quantum Corporation in October, Cassava Technologies in November. SeQure ships zero-trust cybersecurity validated by the Army Analytics Group at 120 million inferences per second on Groq hardware. Maritech Intelligence Systems launched with MIST and MADEN against a Navy, DARPA, NOAA, and ONR heritage inherited through the Applied Ocean Sciences acquisition in January 2026. Six product lines live under one roof. That structure rewards scale and punishes context drift.

II. Thesis

Most organizations solve that problem too late. They automate the wrong layer, then inherit the technical debt for five years. The useful view is simpler. Roughly 60% of orchestration work is traditional code and database plumbing. 30% is rule-based logic. 10% is the genuinely novel AI problem. For Entanglement, the 10% is obvious and well-staffed: the algorithmic core, the quantum-inspired optimization work, the AGI research posture. The 60% and 30% are where holding companies leak value. Context duplication across entities. Proposal writing that restarts each cycle. Engineering onboarding that takes a quarter instead of a month. Orchestration patterns that ship once to one customer and never get reused for the next.

III. Method

There is a methodology for this. Eduba's Interpretable Context Methodology treats agent and organizational context as a layered filesystem. L0 identity, L1 routing, L2 contracts, L3 reusable factory, L4 working artifacts. Each layer has a defined purpose and a defined reader. For a holding company with spin-outs, it maps directly. Parent-level identity and routing at L0/L1. Per-entity contracts and stage gates at L2. Shared reusable assets, proposal libraries, technical narrative patterns, federal documentation scaffolds at L3. Customer deployments and program artifacts at L4. Work moves faster because context is organized, and governance is measurable because the structure is legible. The paper is published and the repository is open: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-, MIT license, submitted to ACM TiiS.
